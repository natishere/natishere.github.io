The Awareness Theory of Everything (ATE) is a foundational theoretical framework proposing that physical structure arises from recursive classification and stability, without introducing new fundamental entities beyond minimal primitives.

ATE addresses particles, fields, time, mass, entanglement, and emergence as consequences of how classification structures persist, propagate, and correlate â€” rather than as independent ontological ingredients.

Overview

ATE begins with a minimal set of primitives describing distinction, opposition, and classification. From these, it derives:

  • Particles as closed recursive classification structures
  • Fields as distributed classification constraints
  • Time as sustained recursive closure
  • Mass as the cost of maintaining classification stability
  • Entanglement as shared classification without spatial mediation
